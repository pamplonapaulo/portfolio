My journalistic hat

Moving foward doesn't mean quitting knowledge!

Despite being anxious to become a master in programming languages, I couldn't say that I regret my Journalism college at all. The impact of it's leassons will always reverberate in my personality and work. My eye had changed, so my opinions too.

As a freelancer web developer, I'm able to give a lot of extra advices to my clients. The less corporative they are, the more they will need the public relations knowledge that I got working with media and journalists under 10 years. You cannot forget that your website is going to be your main public media, your public hub of all information under your control. Be aweare about what you should or shouldn't include there.

I also know pretty well what the media is looking for, so I can give you some help to show them what you have. I can also let you know what you should change in your business in order to not being caught in negative stories.

But at my developer career, this knowledge will be more usefull once I start to compete for senior and management positions, sharing with my bosses my strategic eye about the public audiences.
